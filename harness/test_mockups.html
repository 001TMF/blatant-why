◆ I have a protein called PD-L1 and I want to make something that binds to it. What should I do?

Great question! PD-L1 (Programmed Death-Ligand 1) is one of the most well-studied immuno-oncology targets. There are several approaches to designing a binder:

Your Options

  1. VHH Nanobody — Single-domain antibody (~15 kDa). Smallest, most stable, easiest to engineer. Best for first campaigns.
  2. scFv Antibody — Single-chain variable fragment (~27 kDa). More traditional, larger binding surface.
  3. De novo Miniprotein — Computationally designed binder (65–150 aa). Novel fold, no antibody framework.

Recommended Next Steps

  1. I can research PD-L1 automatically — pull structures from PDB, check UniProt for domains, and search SAbDab for existing nanobodies
  2. Based on that, I will identify the best epitope (binding site) and propose a campaign plan
  3. You review the plan, then I launch the design pipeline on Tamarind Bio cloud compute

For a first experiment, I would recommend a VHH nanobody campaign using the caplacizumab scaffold — it has the highest stability and most design data.
